# Words describing Negativity

## People
```
Malevolent: Having or exhibiting ill will; wishing harm to others.
Vindictive: Disposed to seek revenge; vengeful.
Nefarious: Wicked, villainous, or heinously criminal.
Odious: Extremely unpleasant; repulsive.
Egocentric: Self-centered or self-absorbed
Devious: Showing a skillful use of underhanded tactics; deceitful.
Machiavellian: Cunning, scheming, and unscrupulous in advancing one's interests.
Insidious: Proceeding in a gradual, subtle way with harmful effects.
Pompous: Self-important; displaying arrogant self-importance.
Callous: Emotionally insensitive; showing a cruel disregard for others.
Brazen: Bold and without shame; brash.
Malicious: Intending to do harm; malevolent.
Cynical: Believing that people are motivated purely by self-interest; distrustful of human sincerity.
Arrogant: Having an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities.
```

## Things or Situations
```
Egregious: Outstandingly bad; shocking.
Abhorrent: Inspiring disgust and loathing; repugnant.
Iniquitous: Grossly unfair and morally wrong.
Maleficent: Harmful or evil in intent or effect.
Aberrant: Departing from an accepted standard or normal course.
Preposterous: Contrary to reason or common sense; utterly absurd.
Insidious: Proceeding in a gradual, subtle way with harmful effects.
Malignant: Malevolent or dangerous to health; harmful.
Diabolical: Wicked, devilish, or outrageously wicked.
Noxious: Harmful, poisonous, or very unpleasant.
```

## The Nature and Weather
```
Untamed: Resisting domestication or cultivation; fiercely wild.
Tempestuous: Characterized by fierce and tumultuous emotion; stormy and turbulent.
Arid: Desolately dry, lacking vitality and fertility.
Whirlwind: Chaotic and uncontrollable, creating a sense of instability.
Turbulent: Marked by conflict, disorder, and unrest.
Iced: Overwhelming cold and harsh weather.
Blistering: Extremely hot and scorching, almost unbearable.
Sweltering: Oppressively hot, creating an almost suffocating environment.
Desolate: Eerily empty, giving a sense of abandonment and isolation.
Biting: Piercing and sharp, causing discomfort and unease.
Searing: Excessively hot and scalding, possibly causing harm.
```

# Words describing Positivity

## People
```
Empathetic: Understanding and sharing the feelings of others; compassionate.
Exemplary: Worthy of imitation; serving as a model of excellence.
Noble: Having high moral qualities; honorable and virtuous.
Amiable: Friendly and pleasant; affable.
Sagacious: Wise and discerning; possessing keen perception.
Serene: Calm, peaceful, and untroubled.
Vivacious: Lively and animated; full of energy.
Exquisite: Extremely beautiful and delicate; finely crafted.
Altruistic: Selflessly concerned for the well-being of others; altruistic.
Radiant: Emitting warmth and brilliance; shining brightly.
Gracious: Courteous, kind, and showing good manners.
```

## Things or Situations
```
Exquisite: Of exceptional beauty and charm; delicately crafted.
Majestic: Having grandeur and dignity; impressive in scale.
Ethereal: Delicate and light in a way that seems too perfect for this world.
Stellar: Exceptionally good; outstanding.
Immaculate: Perfectly clean, neat, or tidy; flawless.
Idyllic: Extremely happy, peaceful, or picturesque; like an idealized place.
Flawless: Without any imperfections or defects; perfect.
Harmonious: Forming a harmonious and consistent whole; balanced.
Halcyon: Denoting a period of time in the past that was idyllically happy and peaceful.
Exultant: Triumphantly happy; jubilant and ecstatic.
Breathtaking: Astonishing or awe-inspiring in quality; taking one's breath away.
Euphoric: Intensely happy and confident; elated.
```
